# The Family Digital Safety Checklist

**Subtitle:** 15 things every family should do to stay safe from AI scams, phishing, and identity theft.

**Target audience:** Ohio families, especially those with elderly parents or grandparents.

**Format:** Single-page printable PDF, designed to hang on a fridge or pin to a bulletin board.

---

## Checklist Content

### Phone & Call Safety
- [ ] Set up a family safe word that only real family members know
- [ ] Never send money based on a phone call alone -- always verify by calling back on a known number
- [ ] Register all family phone numbers on the National Do Not Call Registry (donotcall.gov)
- [ ] Enable spam call filtering on all family phones (built into iPhone and Android)
- [ ] Save emergency contacts so you can quickly verify any suspicious call

### Email & Text Safety
- [ ] Never click links in unexpected emails or texts -- type the website address directly
- [ ] Check the sender's email address carefully (scammers use addresses that are one letter off)
- [ ] Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) on all email accounts
- [ ] Report phishing emails by forwarding to reportphishing@apwg.org
- [ ] Teach every family member what a phishing email looks like

### Account & Identity Safety
- [ ] Use a unique, strong password for every account (use a password manager like Bitwarden or 1Password)
- [ ] Enable MFA on banking, social media, and shopping accounts
- [ ] Freeze credit with all three bureaus (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion) -- it's free
- [ ] Check credit reports annually at annualcreditreport.com
- [ ] Set up identity monitoring alerts for all family members
