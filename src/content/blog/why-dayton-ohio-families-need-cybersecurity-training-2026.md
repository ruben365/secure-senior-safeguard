# Why Dayton Ohio Families Need Cybersecurity Training in 2026

If you think cybersecurity is just about passwords and spam emails, 2026 has some bad news for you. The scams hitting families in the Dayton and Kettering area today look and sound nothing like the ones from a few years ago. They use artificial intelligence to clone voices, create fake video calls, and write messages so convincing that even tech-savvy people fall for them.

Cybersecurity training in Dayton, Ohio isn't optional anymore. It's how you protect the people you love.

## 2026 Is Not Like 2020: AI Changed Everything

Remember when the biggest scam risk was a badly written email from a "Nigerian prince"? Those days are long gone.

Today's scammers use AI tools to:

- **Clone a family member's voice** from a short social media clip and call you pretending to be them
- **Write perfect phishing emails** with no spelling mistakes, using details scraped from your Facebook page
- **Create fake texts from your bank** that look identical to real alerts, complete with your account details
- **Run romance scams** with AI-generated photos and conversations that feel genuinely personal
- **Build fake websites** that copy real local businesses down to the last detail

These aren't future threats. They're happening right now in the Miami Valley. The Federal Trade Commission reported that Americans lost over $12 billion to scams in 2025, and AI-powered fraud was the fastest-growing category. Older adults and their families were hit the hardest.

The old advice -- "just don't click suspicious links" -- doesn't cut it anymore. Families need real training that covers what today's scams actually look like.

## Why the Dayton and Kettering Area Is a Target

You might wonder why scammers would focus on a mid-sized metro like Dayton. The answer is simple: communities built on trust are easier to exploit.

Here's what makes our area vulnerable:

### An Aging Population

The Dayton metro area has a higher percentage of residents over 65 than the national average. Scammers know that older adults are more likely to answer phone calls from unknown numbers, less likely to recognize AI-generated content, and more likely to have savings worth stealing.

This isn't about intelligence. It's about exposure. Many seniors didn't grow up with this technology, and the scams are designed to exploit that gap.

### Strong Community Trust

In Kettering, Centerville, Beavercreek, and neighborhoods across Dayton, people trust their neighbors. That's a wonderful thing. But scammers weaponize that trust. They impersonate local churches, community organizations, and even neighbors to make their schemes feel familiar and safe.

We've talked to families right here in the Miami Valley who received [deepfake phone calls](/articles/5-signs-phone-call-is-deepfake) from someone pretending to be a grandchild. The voice sounded real. The story sounded believable. The only thing that stopped them was training.

### A Military and Veteran Community

Wright-Patterson Air Force Base makes Dayton one of the largest military communities in the country. Active duty families and veterans face unique scam risks, including fake VA benefit calls, phony military discount offers, and identity theft targeting service records.

As a veteran-founded company, InVision Network understands these risks firsthand. We built our training with military families in mind.
