## 3. Voice Constants

These stay the same across ALL content:

**Personality:** Warm, protective, competent, local, honest
**Values:** Family safety, community service, accessibility, transparency
**Expertise level:** Deep technical knowledge delivered in plain language
**Perspective:** We've been through it ourselves — we're not lecturers, we're fellow survivors
**Tone baseline:** Like a trusted neighbor who happens to be a cybersecurity expert

**Always:**
- Use "we" and "you" — direct, personal
- Lead with the benefit, not the feature
- Name specific threats (deepfakes, voice cloning, phishing) — don't be vague
- Mention Ohio/Kettering/Dayton naturally when relevant
- End with a clear next step

**Never:**
- Use fear tactics or panic language
- Assume technical knowledge
- Use passive voice when active is possible
- Say "click here" — use descriptive link text
- Exaggerate metrics or invent credentials

## 5. Terminology Guide

### Preferred terms (use these)
- **AI scam protection** (not "cybersecurity services")
- **Families and businesses** (not "consumers and enterprises")
- **Workshops** or **training sessions** (not "courses" or "modules")
- **Voice-clone scams** (not "synthetic voice fraud")
- **Deepfake detection** (specific, not vague "threat analysis")
- **AI receptionist** (not "virtual agent" or "chatbot")
- **AI Insurance** (our branded term for maintenance/support subscriptions)
- **Veteran-founded** (when relevant, don't overuse)
- **Real human support** or **a real person you can call** (not "customer service")
- **Kettering, Ohio** / **Greater Dayton area** / **Southwest Ohio** (natural location mentions)
- **Family safe word** (our recommended security protocol)
- **Same-day setup** / **no contracts** / **30-day guarantee** (trust signals)

### Terms to avoid
- "Cyber" as a standalone adjective
- "Solutions" without context (too generic)
- "Cutting-edge" or "state-of-the-art" (cliché)
- "Revolutionize" or "disrupt" (startup jargon)
- "Click here" (use descriptive anchor text)
- "Dear valued customer" (impersonal)
- "Act now" or "Limited time" (pressure tactics)
- "Enterprise-grade" when talking to families

## 7. CTA Style Guide

CTAs on this site follow a pattern: **verb-first, benefit-oriented, specific.**

| CTA | Context | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| "Get Protected" | Homepage hero | Direct, action-oriented, benefit clear |
| "Start Training" | About page | Verb-first, implies forward motion |
| "See Our Work" | Homepage secondary | Curiosity-driven, low commitment |
| "Get in Touch" | About/Contact | Warm, approachable, not "Contact Us" |
| "Book a Workshop" | Training page | Specific action, clear outcome |
| "Schedule a Free Strategy Call" | Business page | Specific, free, no pressure |
| "Explore Our Workshops" | AnswerSummary | Discovery-oriented, inviting |
| "Talk to a Human" | CTA sections | Differentiator — emphasizes real people |
| "Start Protecting My Family" | Final CTA | Personal ("my"), emotional, specific |

**CTA rules:**
- Start with a verb (Get, Start, Book, Explore, Talk, Call)
- Name the benefit or outcome when possible
- Use "my" or "your" to make it personal
- Keep under 5 words when possible
- Never use "Submit" for form buttons — use the specific action ("Send Message", "Book Now")
